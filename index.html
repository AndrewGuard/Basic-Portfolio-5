About Me

I started out studying Art History and French at UC Berkeley, but over the years my career trajectory moved steadily into Information Technology. In my initial professional jobs, I couldn't help but start improving existing processes and redesigning workflows wherever I went. Naturally, I eventually moved into doing this for clients full-time as an analyst. In my current work as a Sr. Business Analyst at Northwestern University, I research, design and deploy technical solutions for offices and departments across campus and contribute code to systems in production. My master's thesis at the University of Chicago in Medical and Psychological Anthropology centered on my conceptual design for a new patient portal system that would target and improve health equity for patients with lower levels of literacy. Health IT has become a passion of mine and health information systems a growing area of personal research. In my spare time, I'm an avid classical pianist. A better word might be obsessed. I still love all things French and ancient art and archaeology will always be some of my mainstay fascinations.
